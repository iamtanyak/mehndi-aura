London-area bookings

Mehndi for weddings, Eid and family celebrations.

Different days need different kinds of mehndi.

For brides

Fuller designs, calmer planning, and time to talk through coverage, outfit details, jewellery and photos.

For Eid

Pretty hands for the morning rush: clean florals, tiny trails, jewellery details or something simple and sweet.

For parties

Guest designs, sangeet nights, birthdays and family events where the mehndi should look lovely without slowing everything down.

Travel notes

Tell me the area first, then I can be realistic.

I welcome London-area enquiries, and I’m happy to look at wider UK bookings too. Travel depends on the date, distance, booking size and how long the design work will take, so the location is one of the first things I check before confirming anything.

  • Send the city, venue or postcode area when you enquire
  • Tell me if it is for a bride, Eid, a party or a group booking
  • Share a few design references if you have them
  • I’ll reply with honest timing, travel and next steps
Before you message

A few things people usually ask me.

Can I ask even if I am not sure what design I want?

Yes, please do. Many people only know the feeling they want: soft, full, minimal, floral, Arabic-inspired or jewellery-like. We can work from there.

Do you take bridal bookings?

Yes. For bridal mehndi, I like to understand the outfit, coverage, timing and any personal details before giving a proper quote.

What should I send before booking?

Send your date, area or venue, event type, guest count, and any photos you have saved. If anything is missing, I’ll ask.

Does Tanya use black henna?

No. I do not offer black henna. I prefer natural henna-style work and clear aftercare advice so the experience feels safe and calm.
